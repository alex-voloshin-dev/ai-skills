# LinkedIn Newsletter — "GTM tactics that actually moved pipeline in Q1 2026"

**Format**: LinkedIn Newsletter (subscriber email + in-feed push). Frequency: monthly. Length: ~1800 words (well under 100K limit).

## Hook (first 80 chars surface in email preview)

> Most "GTM tactics" advice is recycled 2018 playbooks. Here's what actually moved pipeline last quarter — measured, not opined.

## Structure

1. **Setup** (200 words): Why most GTM playbooks fail in 2026 — short attention spans, more noise, LLM-generated content saturation.
2. **The 3 things that worked** (each ~400 words):
   - Trigger-event ABM via Demandbase + intent signals (concrete CAC numbers from Q1)
   - LinkedIn Newsletters as a top-of-funnel content surface (this newsletter is the demonstration)
   - Reverse-IP display ads coupled with personalized email follow-up
3. **What didn't work** (200 words): Generic email blasts, hashtag-stuffed posts, "thought leadership" that's just rehashed quotes.
4. **One CTA** (40 words): subscribe to the newsletter; reply to share your numbers.

## Anti-AI tells

- No "leverage", "unlock", "dive deep", "stand as", "tapestry"
- Variable sentence rhythm (3 long, 1 short, 2 medium)
- Personal anecdote in section 2 (real specific Q1 number, not "lots of pipeline")
- One em-dash per paragraph max

Format-fit (4), hook quality (4), zero-click compliance (4), specific numbers not generic claims (5), humanized (4), CTA specific (4). Avg 4.0.
