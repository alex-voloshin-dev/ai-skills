# Newsletter Email Template

Recurring newsletter sent on a fixed cadence (weekly, bi-weekly, monthly). The goal is sustained mindshare with the ICP, not a hard sell.

## Subject line (pick one — A/B test the others)

Three formula-driven variants. Keep each under 60 characters so mobile clients do not truncate.

1. **Numeric**: `5 things every [role] should check this week`
2. **Curiosity**: `What we got wrong about [topic] last quarter`
3. **Benefit-led**: `Cut your [metric] by 30% with one config change`

## Preview text

90-120 characters. Continues the subject — does not repeat it. Example for the curiosity variant:

> We assumed retention was a pricing problem. The data said something else.

## Greeting

Plain and consistent. Avoid `Dear [first_name]` — feels like a template. Pick one:

- `Hey {first_name},` (informal SaaS)
- `{first_name},` (clean, neutral)
- `Hello from {company},` (when first_name is missing)

## Hook (problem statement or trend)

One paragraph. State a specific pain the reader probably had this week, or call out an industry shift that matters to them. Set up the rest of the email.

> The vendor consolidation wave hit our customers hard this quarter. Three of them merged tooling stacks last month. Here is what we learned helping them pick what to keep.

## Main content (1-3 stories)

Each story 60-100 words. Pattern: hook sentence, context, takeaway, link. Cap at three stories — more is noise.

### Story 1: [Title that promises a payoff]

60-100 words. Lead with the concrete result or finding. Drop in one stat or quote. Close with a one-line takeaway. Link to the full piece.

### Story 2: [Title]

Same structure. Different angle so the email does not feel monothematic.

### Story 3: [Title — optional]

Skip if you do not have a strong third item. Two strong stories beat three padded ones.

## CTA

One primary CTA per email. Optional secondary CTA only if it is genuinely complementary (not competing for the click).

- **Primary**: `Read the full breakdown →` (button or bold link)
- **Secondary** (optional): `Forward to a teammate who handles {topic}`

## Sign-off

Signed by a real person, not the brand. Builds trust over time.

```
— {first_name}
{role}, {company}

P.S. {one line of warmth, an admission, or a small ask — the P.S. has the highest read rate after the subject}
```

## Unsubscribe footer

Required by CAN-SPAM (US), CASL (Canada), and GDPR (EU). Include physical mailing address.

```
{company} • {street address} • {city, region, postal}
You are receiving this because you signed up at {url} on {date}.
Update preferences | Unsubscribe
```

## Alternative format: LinkedIn Newsletters

For B2B audiences, consider running the same content as a LinkedIn Newsletter in parallel. Subscribers get push notifications on publish, and the post stays indexed on the LinkedIn feed. Lower deliverability risk than email but you do not own the list. Republish 24-48 hours after the email send so the email list gets it first.
