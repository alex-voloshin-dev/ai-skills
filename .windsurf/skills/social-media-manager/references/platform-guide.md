# Platform Guide — 2026 Algorithm Rules & Post Formats

Last updated: 2026-03-23

This guide is based on research of current 2026 algorithm behavior across
all three platforms. Algorithm rules change — re-research quarterly.

**Note**: Replace `your-domain.com` with the actual product domain from
`marketing/MARKETING.md` when writing posts.

---

## X / Twitter

### Algorithm rules (2026)

- **External links**: 30-50% reach reduction. Non-Premium accounts posting
  links see ZERO median engagement since March 2026.
- **Link-in-reply** partially works for Premium accounts. For free accounts,
  even reply links get suppressed.
- **Images/videos**: +40% engagement over text-only tweets.
- **Threads** (4-8 tweets): best format for educational B2B content.
  1-2 threads per week recommended.
- **Hashtags**: 1-2 max. More hurts reach. Some data suggests zero is fine.
- **Personal accounts**: 5x more engagement than company pages.
- **Best posting times**: Tuesday-Thursday mornings (audience timezone).
- **Engagement signals**: replies > retweets > likes. Prompt replies.

### Post format

```
## Main tweet (≤280 chars, no links, no hashtags)

[Hook: surprising finding, question, or concrete action]
[Bridge: 1-2 sentences building tension or curiosity]
[Soft CTA: "try it yourself" / invitation, not a hard sell]

## Reply 1 (the substance)
[Prompts, data, method — the actionable part]

## Reply 2 (the insight)
[The non-obvious takeaway that makes people think]

## Reply 3 (optional, the CTA)
[Blog mention as plain text, "your-domain.com", engagement question]
```

### What to avoid on X

- Don't put links in the main tweet. Ever.
- Don't use 🧵 emoji on every thread (it's become a cliché).
  Use it sparingly or not at all.
- Don't start every hook with "[Bold claim]. Here's [what/how]."
  Vary the structure: question, action, observation, data point.
- Don't write threads that read like compressed blog posts.
  Each tweet should feel like a standalone thought.

### Hook formulas that work (rotate, don't repeat)

1. **The experiment**: "We did [specific thing]. Here's what happened."
2. **The question**: "Do you know what [platform] says about your [thing]?"
3. **The contrast**: "[Thing A] says X. [Thing B] says the opposite."
4. **The action**: "Open [tool] and type [prompt]. What do you see?"
5. **The data**: "We scanned [N] sites. [Surprising stat]."

### Engagement prompts (end of thread)

Don't use generic "What do you think?" — be specific:
- "What did you find when you checked yours?"
- "Which LLM got your brand right?"
- "Have you looked at your robots.txt recently?"

## LinkedIn

### Algorithm rules (2026)

- **External links**: -60% organic reach. This is the biggest penalty.
- **Link-in-comments**: also penalized. LinkedIn detects "bridge behavior"
  (posting without link, then adding link in first comment).
- **Zero-click content**: deliver full value in the post. This is the
  winning strategy for 2026 LinkedIn.
- **Saves**: 5x more powerful than likes as an engagement signal.
  Create reference-worthy content (prompts, checklists, frameworks).
- **Comments**: 15x more weight than likes. Ask specific questions.
- **Dwell time**: how long people spend reading your post. Longer posts
  that hold attention rank higher.
- **Optimal post length**: 1,300-1,900 characters (47% more engagement
  than shorter posts).
- **First 140-235 characters**: visible before "See more" fold. The hook
  must be compelling enough to click "See more".
- **Personal profiles**: 561% more reach than company pages.
- **Document/carousel posts**: 3x higher engagement than text-only.
- **Hashtags**: 3-5, CamelCase format. More than 5 hurts reach.
- **Best posting times**: Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10am audience timezone.
- **Consistency**: posting on the same days/times trains the algorithm.

### Post format

```
## Post (1,300-1,900 chars, zero-click, no external links)

[Hook: 1 line, visible before "See more" fold. About the READER, not you.]

[Personal angle: "We tested...", "I checked...", "When we scanned..."]

[The finding: specific, surprising, with names and numbers]

[Method / actionable content with → arrows:]
→ [Item 1 — what it does]
→ [Item 2 — what it does]
→ [Item 3 — what it does]

[Soft CTA: mention domain as plain text, "your-domain.com"]

[Engagement question: specific, not generic]

[Hashtags: 3-5, CamelCase]
```

### What to avoid on LinkedIn

- Don't put clickable URLs in the post body. Mention "your-domain.com"
  as plain text or say "link in profile".
- Don't write from the company page. Personal profiles get 561% more reach.
- Don't open with "I'm excited to announce" or "I'm thrilled to share".
- Don't write walls of text without visual breaks. Use → arrows, line breaks.
- Don't end with "Thoughts?" — it's generic. Ask a specific question.

### Hook formulas that work on LinkedIn

1. **The test**: "We tested [thing] across [N platforms]. [Result]."
2. **The blind spot**: "[Audience], when was the last time you checked [thing]?"
3. **The contrast**: "[Expected thing] vs [actual thing]. They're not the same."
4. **The number**: "[Stat] of sites we scanned [had this problem]."
5. **The definition**: "[Term] = [short definition]. Not [common confusion]."

### Alternative formats (higher engagement)

**Carousel / document post** (3x engagement):
- Slide 1: hook question or bold statement (cover)
- Slides 2-4: the content (one idea per slide)
- Slide 5: CTA + "your-domain.com"
- Save as PDF, upload as document post

**Poll** (high engagement, use sparingly):
- Ask a question relevant to the product's problem space
- Options should include: manual approach / tool approach / "I don't" / awareness check

## Facebook

### Algorithm rules (2026)

- **Organic reach**: ~1.65% per post. Lowest of all platforms.
- **External links**: heavily penalized by Meta (same as LinkedIn).
- **Video**: Meta strongly biased toward short-form vertical video.
  Video gets dramatically more organic reach than text posts.
- **Meaningful Social Interactions (MSI)**: long comments, shares to
  Stories, and watch time matter more than likes.
- **Groups**: where Facebook works for B2B. Page posts have minimal reach.
- **Hashtags**: 2-4 max. Less important than on other platforms.

### Post format (text)

```
## Post (short, conversational, no external links)

[Hook: relatable question or observation, 1-2 lines]

[Finding: specific, with names, in 2-3 short sentences]

[Action: one simple thing the reader can try right now]

[Soft CTA: "your-domain.com" as plain text]

[Hashtags: 2-4]
```

### Video format (recommended for better reach)

Short-form video (30-60 seconds) performs 5-10x better than text on Facebook.

**Screen recording approach**:
- Record yourself using the product to demonstrate a real result
- Show a before/after or a surprising finding
- End with CTA: "Check yours at [your domain]"

**Post text for video**: keep it to 1-2 lines. The video does the talking.

### Strategic consideration

Facebook's organic B2B reach is very low. Consider whether each post is
worth the effort, or if the time would be better spent on a second LinkedIn
post or more X engagement. Facebook works best for:
- Sharing in relevant groups (SEO groups, digital marketing groups)
- Video content (screen recordings, comparison videos)
- Retargeting via paid ads (if budget allows)

For text-only blog promotion with zero following, the ROI is minimal.
Be honest about this in recommendations.

## Cross-platform rules

### Post scheduling

| Platform | Best days | Best times | Frequency |
|----------|-----------|------------|-----------|
| X/Twitter | Tue-Thu | Morning | 1-2 posts/day, 1-2 threads/week |
| LinkedIn | Tue-Thu | 8-10am | 1 post/day |
| Facebook | Wed-Fri | 1-3pm | 2-3x/week (if worth it) |

### Content adaptation (not copy-paste)

Each platform needs a different version, not a resized copy:
- **X**: Punchiest hook, thread for depth, engagement prompt
- **LinkedIn**: Professional peer angle, zero-click full value, → arrows
- **Facebook**: Shortest, most casual, video preferred

### Naming convention

Files: `marketing/posts/YYYY-MM-DD-[platform]-[slug].md`

Examples:
- `2026-03-23-x-check-chatgpt-recommends.md`
- `2026-03-23-linkedin-check-chatgpt-recommends.md`
- `2026-03-23-facebook-check-chatgpt-recommends.md`
