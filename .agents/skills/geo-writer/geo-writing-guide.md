## Why This Guide Exists

Classic SEO traffic is projected to drop ~25% by end of 2026 as AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and Claude answer users directly. For any content-driven project, every public-facing piece must be built to be *cited and quoted* by LLMs — not only ranked in blue links.

This guide consolidates recommendations from 2026 practitioner sources. Treat it as the baseline checklist before any public-facing content is published or refreshed.

---

## 1. Core Principles

1. **Write for extraction, not just for reading.** LLMs lift 30-180 word chunks. Every section must be self-contained enough to be quoted on its own.
2. **Answer first, context second.** The first sentence of each H2/H3 must fully answer the implied question in 30-60 words. Supporting detail follows.
3. **One topic per section.** One H2 = one entity or question. Do not merge sub-topics — it blocks chunk-level extraction.
4. **Entity-first, not keyword-first.** Name the product, brand, person, or concept explicitly; never use pronouns across paragraphs. LLMs parse entities, not keyword density.
5. **Verifiable > persuasive.** Every non-trivial claim needs a statistic, date, source, or named example. Opinion-heavy prose is ignored by retrieval systems.

---

## 2. Structure Rules (Macro / Meso / Micro)

Based on the GEO-SFE framework (arXiv 2603.29979): applying these three levels consistently raises citation rate by ~17% and subjective quality by ~18% across six mainstream AI engines.

### Macro-structure — document architecture
- Single H1 that states the question or topic literally. No clever wordplay.
- 5-9 H2 sections. Each H2 should read as a standalone question or declarative topic ("What is a GEO score?", not "The score").
- Table of contents at the top for articles over 1,200 words.
- Conclusion section named `TL;DR` or `Key takeaways` with 3-5 bullet points — this is the chunk most frequently cited.

### Meso-structure — information chunking
- Paragraphs: 40-80 words. Hard cap at ~120 words per paragraph.
- Sections: target 120-180 words per H2 block. Aligns with typical AI response length.
- One idea per paragraph. If you use `however`, `but`, or `on the other hand`, split into two paragraphs.
- Insert a statistic, named source, or concrete example every 150-200 words.

### Micro-structure — visual emphasis
- Bold the 3-5 most quotable phrases per section. LLMs over-weight bolded spans during chunking.
- Bullet lists for enumerations (3+ items). Numbered lists for ordered processes.
- Tables for any comparison of 2+ entities across 2+ attributes. Comparison tables are cited at ~26% higher rate than equivalent prose.

## 3. Format Patterns That Get Cited

Ranked by observed citation lift:

| Pattern | Why it works | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| Comparison tables | Clean rows/columns, direct extraction | Tools, pricing tiers, competitors, features |
| FAQ blocks with matching JSON-LD | 3.1-3.2× higher extraction vs plain prose | End of every pillar page |
| How-to numbered steps | Maps cleanly to HowTo schema | Tutorials, onboarding, fix-it posts |
| Definition + example pairs | Matches the "X is Y" retrieval pattern | Glossary, concept explainers |
| Named statistics with year | `+22% AI visibility in 2026` is quotable | Intro and body of any thought-leadership post |
| Direct quotes from named experts | Quotation patterns boost visibility ~37% | Trend posts, research summaries |

Comparison articles lead citation rates at ~32.5% among all content formats.

## 4. The Answer-First Template

Use this template for every H2/H3 section:

```
## <Question phrased as H2>

<30-60 word direct answer, no preamble.>

<Optional 1-2 supporting paragraphs, 40-80 words each, with at least one number or named source.>

<Optional bullet list, table, or code block.>
```

Example (do):
> ## What is an AI-Readiness Score?
>
> **An AI-Readiness Score is a 0-100 rating that measures how easily AI systems — ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Gemini — can read, understand, and cite a website.** It is generated by the tool by scanning the site against 30+ GEO parameters, including schema coverage, chunk structure, entity clarity, and content freshness.

Anti-pattern (don't):
> ## Understanding the Score
>
> When it comes to AI search, there are many things to consider. In this section, we'll explore what makes a site truly "AI-ready" and why it matters…

## 5. Schema / JSON-LD Requirements

Schema markup raises AI citation rates by 30%+ on average. Every published page must ship at least the mandatory schemas below.

### Mandatory on every page
- **Organization** — on homepage and via `sameAs` on every article. Link to Wikipedia (if available), LinkedIn, Crunchbase, GitHub, X.
- **WebSite** — with `potentialAction` for sitelinks search.
- **BreadcrumbList** — on every non-homepage URL.

### Mandatory by content type
| Content type | Required schema | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Blog / article | `Article` + `Person` (author) | Include `datePublished`, `dateModified`, `author.sameAs` |
| Pillar / guide with Q&A | `Article` + `FAQPage` | Questions must literally match prompt phrasing |
| Tutorial / walkthrough | `HowTo` | `step` array with 3-8 steps |
| Product / tool page | `Product` or `SoftwareApplication` | Include `aggregateRating` if honest data exists |
| Glossary entry | `DefinedTerm` within `DefinedTermSet` | |

### FAQPage — the single highest-leverage schema
- 4-8 questions per page, each 8-20 words.
- Answers: 40-80 words, self-contained, matching the prompt phrasing.
- Do not duplicate marketing copy — write answers as if teaching a beginner.
- Test in Google Rich Results Test and `schema.org/validator` before merge.

### Author and E-E-A-T triad
Organization + Person + Article together form the entity graph that AI systems use to verify publisher credibility. Every author must have:
- Dedicated author page with `Person` JSON-LD.
- `sameAs` pointing to LinkedIn + at least one other platform (X, GitHub, Mastodon).
- Visible human-readable bio at the top or bottom of the article.

## 6. Entities and Brand Signals

Off-site brand mentions correlate with AI visibility at 0.67 — stronger than any on-site factor. Brand mentions beat backlinks 3:1 for AI Overviews presence.

Rules (apply with the project's canonical terms from `marketing/MARKETING.md`):
- **Use the exact canonical brand form.** Match capitalization, spacing, and punctuation of the official brand spelling. No variant acronyms in public content.
- **Use the canonical category name** consistently. Do not invent new category labels per article.
- **Link first mention of any external entity** to its canonical page (Wikipedia, official docs). This trains the entity graph.
- **Use canonical metric and feature names** as hyphenated compound adjectives where applicable. Never switch forms mid-article.
- **Internal links**: every pillar page must interlink to glossary terms and at least 3 related articles.

## 7. Freshness Rules

Pages not refreshed quarterly lose AI citations at ~3× the normal rate.

| Content type | Minimum refresh cadence | Signal to update |
|---|---|---|
| Time-sensitive (news, algo updates, LLM releases) | 24-48 hours | New model version, Google core update, major GEO news |
| FAQ on pillar pages | Monthly | New common question detected in support / sales |
| Evergreen guides | Quarterly | Refresh dates, stats, examples; bump `dateModified` |
| Glossary | Quarterly | New term emerging |
| Landing / commercial pages | Quarterly | Pricing, positioning, case study rotation |

On every refresh:
- Update `dateModified` in Article schema.
- Add a visible "Updated on YYYY-MM-DD" line under the H1.
- Replace at least one stat or example with a fresher one.
- Record change in `marketing/content-calendar.md` (if project maintains one).

For Perplexity specifically, updates within the last 2-3 days materially improve citation odds.

## 8. Platform Differentiation Cheat Sheet

| Engine | Dominant source | Optimization lever |
|---|---|---|
| ChatGPT | Wikipedia (~48% of citations), training data | Semantic authority, long-lived authoritative pages, Wikipedia entity parity |
| Perplexity | Reddit (~47%) + live web | Freshness (2-3 days), Reddit presence, clear dates |
| Google AI Overviews | Organic top 10 (99% of citations) | Classic SEO + schema; if you're not top-10, you're not cited |
| Gemini | YouTube + Google knowledge graph | Video content with chapters, Knowledge Panel claiming |
| Claude | Curated authoritative sources, epistemic rigor | Cite your own sources inline; neutral, evidence-based tone |

**Off-site portfolio** (required for any brand-building topic):
- Reddit: 1 high-quality post or thoughtful comment in the relevant subreddit per pillar topic.
- YouTube: embed a 3-8 min video with descriptive chapters on every pillar page. Chapters act as headers for LLM extraction. YouTube overtook Reddit as the #1 LLM citation source in October 2025 (~16% vs ~10%).
- LinkedIn: 1 long-form post per pillar for B2B visibility.

Stop treating Reddit as the only lever — it is plateauing as a citation source while YouTube grows.

## 10. Anti-Patterns

Do not publish content that contains any of the following:

- **Burying the lede.** No "In this post we will explore…" openings.
- **Pronoun drift.** Do not start a paragraph with `It`, `This`, `They` referring to entities from earlier sections.
- **Walls of text.** Paragraphs over 120 words block chunking.
- **Opinion without a named source.** "Experts say" is invisible to RAG. Name the expert or the study.
- **Duplicate FAQ between the article body and the FAQPage schema.** LLMs de-duplicate aggressively; you waste citation surface.
- **Un-dated statistics.** `"studies show +30%"` with no year or link will not be quoted.
- **AI-generated filler.** Shallow "-ing" analyses, inflated symbolism, empty tricolons. Run text through the `humanizer` skill before publishing.
- **Changing terminology mid-article.** `GEO score` in one paragraph and `AI-readiness rating` in the next confuses the entity graph.
